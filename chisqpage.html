Does an artist's gender have any impact on what art department their work belongs to at the museum?

To answer that, a chi-squared test was used. It checks for statistical dependency between two catagorical variables - gender and art department in our case.

H0 for the chi-squared test: The two variables are statistically independent.

For a false H0, at least some of observed and expected frequencies (the count expected in a cell if the variables were independent) tend not to be close, leading to large residual values (difference between observed and expected values), and a large chi-square statistic.

Obtained chi-square statistic = 189.42

p-value = ~0

A large chi-square statistic with a p-value < 0.05 suggests strong evidence against H0. So, it was rejected with a conclusion that artist gender and museum art department are statistically dependent.

But how strong is the dependency? A residual analysis was performed for that.

The table below shows the standard residuals (residuals over standard error) for the two variables:

Six standard residuals have absolute values greater than 2. Among these, four have values exceeding 3

After observing the positive and negative standardized residuals, the conclusion is that males are more likely to have their artwork in the Architecture & Design department. Conversely, they are less likely to have it in the Drawings & Prints department and even less likely to have it in the Media & Performance department than what one would expect if the variables were truly independent.

Non-males are more likely to have their artwork in the Drawings & Prints department, and even more likely to have it in the Media & Performance department. Conversely, they are less likely to have it in the Architecture & Design department than what one would expect if the variables were truly independent.
